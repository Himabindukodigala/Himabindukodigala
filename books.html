Wings of Fire

Discription

“Wings of Fire” is the autobiography of Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, a renowned Indian scientist and former President of India. The book covers his early life, education, and work in Indian space research and missile programs, detailing his personal and professional struggles. It also narrates the development of missiles like Agni, Prithvi, Akash, Trishul, and Nag, which have made India a significant missile power internationally1234. Dr. Kalam’s story is one of rising from humble beginnings to achieving great heights through determination and hard work.

James by Percival Everett

Discription

“James” is a novel by Percival Everett, published in 2024. It is a reimagining of Mark Twain’s classic, “The Adventures of Huckleberry Finn,” but told from the perspective of Jim, who is an escaped slave. In this retelling, Jim becomes a runaway after learning he will be sold and separated from his family. The story follows his escape to an island and his subsequent adventures with Huckleberry “Huck” Finn, who has faked his own death to escape his abusive fathe.

The Women by Kristin Hannah

Discription

“The Women” is a historical fiction novel by Kristin Hannah, published in 2024. It tells the story of Frances “Frankie” McGrath, a young nurse serving in the United States Army Nurse Corps during the Vietnam War. The novel explores her journey from naivety to becoming a skilled OR nurse and addresses the challenges faced by women veterans with PTSD upon their return to society in the late 1960s and early 1970s.

All the Worst Humans by Phil Elwood

Discription

“All the Worst Humans” is a candid memoir by Phil Elwood, who was a top PR operative in Washington. In this book, Elwood exposes the secrets of the PR industry, a $129-billion industry that significantly influences what we see and hear in the media. He shares gritty details of his career, where he made news for dictators, tycoons, and politicians, and now aims to pull back the curtain on the industry’s control over public perception. The book is noted for its disarmingly funny and entertaining narrative style, offering a unique perspective on the corridors of power.

The Ministry of Time by Kaliane Bradley

Discription

“The Ministry of Time” by Kaliane Bradley is an exhilarating debut novel that combines elements of a time travel romance, a spy thriller, and a workplace comedy. It offers an ingenious exploration of the nature of truth and power, and the potential for love to change everything. The story revolves around a civil servant who is offered a high-paying job at a government ministry that is experimenting with time travel, working with individuals from different historical periods.

Martyr! by Kaveh Akbar

Discription

“Martyr!” by Kaveh Akbar is a novel that explores the search for meaning in life through various lenses such as faith, art, and personal relationships. The protagonist is a newly sober, orphaned son of Iranian immigrants, who embarks on a journey guided by the voices of artists, poets, and kings. His search leads him to a terminally ill painter in the Brooklyn Museum, weaving a narrative of inheritance, violence, loss, and the discovery of a family secret.

Nuclear War by Annie Jacobsen

Discription

“Nuclear War: A Scenario” by Annie Jacobsen is a nonfiction book that presents a hypothetical timeline of a first strike against the United States by North Korea. The book offers a detailed account of America’s nuclear arsenal’s growth post-World War II and explores battle plans like the Single Integrated Operational Plan (SIOP) that outlined methods for total devastation. Jacobsen’s work is a play-by-play exercise in gaming an apocalyptic war, highlighting the ever-present threat of nuclear holocaust.

All the Colors of the Dark by Chris Whitaker

Discription

“All the Colors of the Dark” by Chris Whitaker is set in 1975 Missouri and follows the story of 13-year-old orphan Saint Brown and her classmate, Patch Macauley. The narrative spans decades, combining elements of a serial killer thriller with a poignant love story. The plot revolves around girls disappearing in a small town, and Patch, an unlikely hero with an eye patch, who saves a girl from a wealthy family, leaving behind a trail of heartache. The book explores themes of change, heroism, and the fine line between triumph and tragedy.

Lies and Weddings by Kevin Kwan

Discription

“Lies and Weddings” by Kevin Kwan is a story that unfolds amid a lavish Hawaiian wedding, where a forbidden affair leads to dramatic events. The book is described as a hilarious, sophisticated, and thrillingly plotted tale that intertwines love, money, murder, sex, and the lies we tell about them all. It takes readers on a globetrotting journey from Hawaii to Marrakech, Los Angeles, and England’s oldest family estates, offering a mix of romance, humor, and social critique.

Love and the Divine Burden of Numbers: A Novel by Abraham Chang

Discription

“Love and the Divine Burden of Numbers: A Novel” by Abraham Chang is a heartfelt exploration of love and identity. The protagonist, Young Wang, navigates his sophomore year of college while reflecting on his past loves and his current relationship with Erena. The novel is a love letter to Western pop culture and Eastern traditions, set against the backdrop of being a first-generation New Yorker. It’s described as tender, funny, and packed with cultural references, offering insights into making one’s way in an ever-changing world.
